# Testing Protocol (Greenyer / MFMP Style)

This protocol is derived directly from the design conversation and Bob Greenyer's public ULTR and cavitation work. It emphasizes radical documentation, low-cost repeatability, and safety.

## Phase 1: Low-Pressure Air Characterization (Mandatory First Step)

**Purpose**: Verify beautiful, uniform inward spiral flow and HVAT behavior before introducing liquid.

- Connect regulated compressed air (0.1 – 1.0 bar) to the nozzle manifold.
- Observe and video the spiral through the clear viewport at multiple flow rates.
- Record:
  - Inlet pressure
  - Approximate exit velocity (anemometer or high-speed video)
  - HVAT rotation speed (tachometer or video) with brake fully open vs. lightly applied
- Look for any asymmetry, turbulence, or whistling (indicates a bad gap or nozzle alignment).
- Photograph/video the exit jet with and without HVAT spinning.

This phase alone is extremely valuable for understanding the device and produces beautiful footage.

## Phase 2: Basic Hydrodynamic Cavitation (Water + Foil)

1. Mount the device vertically above (or beside) a simple transparent cavitation chamber.
2. Use the 8 mm or 10 mm orifice insert initially.
3. Fill chamber with distilled water + small aluminum foil witness sample (classic Greenyer ULTR signature test).
4. Start at very low flow (just enough to produce visible bubbles).
5. Gradually increase flow / pressure while observing bubble cloud behavior through the chamber walls or viewport.
6. Run for 5–30 minutes. Document:
   - Temperature rise (or lack thereof)
   - Visible pitting/erosion patterns on foil ("snakes", craters, microspheres)
   - Gas evolution
   - Any light emission (sonoluminescence) in dark conditions

## Phase 3: Hybrid ULTR (Ultrasonic + Conditioned Injection)

This is the most promising configuration according to the conversation.

- Add a 40 kHz, 50–100 W piezoelectric transducer (cheap ultrasonic cleaner driver + horn, or dedicated lab sonicator) to the bottom or side of the chamber.
- Pre-saturate water with argon (brighter sonoluminescence) or use deuterium-enriched water for LENR-leaning runs.
- Use the HVAT brake to tune residual swirl — many experimenters report that controlled vorticity helps stabilize certain effects.
- Run controlled matrix:
  - With/without platter device (baseline comparison)
  - Different orifice sizes
  - HVAT braked vs. free vs. driven
  - Different dissolved gases
  - Ultrasonic power levels

## Phase 4: Advanced / LENR-Interest Runs (with caution)

- Deuterium gas sparging or D₂O
- Witness materials: Pd, Ti, Cu, Ni foils or powders (post-run SEM/EDX or mass-spec)
- Neutron / gamma monitoring + proper shielding
- Long-duration runs with data logging (pressure, temperature, radiation)

## Documentation Requirements (MFMP Spirit)

- High-speed or 4K video of bubble behavior and foil changes
- Before/after photos of every witness
- Raw pressure, temperature, and radiation logs (timestamped)
- Exact parameters (flow rate or pressure, HVAT RPM if measured, ultrasonic power, gas type, orifice size, gap setting)
- SEM/EDX or mass-spec results when available

Share everything publicly on LENR-Forum, MFMP channels, or your own site. This device was built to make replication easier and data higher quality.

## Safety Notes

- Compressed air or gas cylinders: proper regulators and burst protection.
- Deuterium: flammable — use in small quantities with good ventilation.
- Ultrasonic + high voltage drivers: electrical safety.
- If pursuing radiation claims: use appropriate detectors and shielding. Treat any positive results with extreme skepticism until independently replicated.
